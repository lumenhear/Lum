Your Questions, Answered

Early intervention is key. Speech therapy can begin as early as 18-24 months if a developmental delay is suspected. The sooner a child gets help, the better the prognosis and long-term outcomes.

Common signs include frequently asking people to repeat themselves, turning up the TV volume louder than others prefer, and difficulty hearing in noisy environments. A comprehensive audiological evaluation at our clinic is the definitive way to know for sure.
